4.1 Emergency Preparedness

  • Emergency Kit: Assemble a kit with essentials such as non-perishable food, water, medications, flashlight, batteries, and a first-aid kit. Regularly check and update the contents.
  • Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan, including meeting points, communication strategies, and evacuation routes. Practice the plan regularly to ensure everyone is familiar with it.


4.2 Energy Conservation

  • During Outages: Use candles and battery-powered devices to conserve energy. Avoid opening refrigerators and freezers to maintain cold temperatures. Consider alternative cooking methods such as camping stoves.
  • Preparation: Invest in backup power sources such as generators, solar-powered chargers, or portable power banks. Ensure they are maintained and ready for use.


4.3 Communication Plans

  • Alternative Methods: Use walkie-talkies, landlines, or pre-arranged signals to communicate during outages. Establish a communication plan with family and friends.
  • Emergency Contacts: Maintain a list of emergency contacts, including family members, neighbors, and local authorities. Share this list with your household and keep it easily accessible.


4.4 Transportation Alternatives

  • Public Transport: Keep up-to-date with service status and have alternate routes planned. Use public transportation apps and websites for real-time information.
  • Private Transport: Ensure your vehicle is well-maintained and have a fuel reserve. Keep a car emergency kit with essentials such as spare tires, tools, and a first-aid kit.
  • Bicycles and Walking: Consider non-motorized transportation options in case of vehicle failures. Ensure you have appropriate gear and know safe routes.
