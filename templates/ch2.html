2.1 Communication Glitches

  • Symptoms: Dropped calls, slow internet speeds, and loss of satellite signals. These issues can happen unexpectedly and may last for extended periods.
  • Consequences: Hindered communication affects personal interactions, professional activities, and emergency response capabilities. It can lead to isolation and difficulty in accessing critical information.

2.2 Power Outages

  • Symptoms: Unexplained blackouts, brownouts, and electrical surges. These outages can be localized or widespread, affecting entire regions.
  • Consequences: Power outages disrupt daily activities, pose safety hazards, and lead to significant economic losses. They can also affect critical infrastructure such as hospitals and emergency services.


2.3 Infrastructure Failures

  • Symptoms: Malfunctions in transportation systems, public utilities, and essential services. These failures can cause significant disruptions to daily life.
  • Consequences: Travel disruptions, compromised safety, and interrupted services impact individuals and communities. Infrastructure failures can also lead to secondary issues such as water shortages and waste management problems.


2.4 Psychological Effects

  • Symptoms: Increased levels of stress, anxiety, and fear within the population. These feelings are often exacerbated by the uncertainty and unpredictability of the threat.
